Project Proposal

Background and Motivation

As avid fans of reality TV, we are fascinated by the phenomenon that is the Bachelor/Bachelorette universe. Each season, millions of people create brackets and even bet money on the contestants and their outcomes. Using previous years’ Bachelor data, we hope to give people actionable insights and trends for their brackets and gossip among friends. Although on the surface analyzing reality TV may seem purely trivial, we hope to use this data to provide insights to help win your bracket or find your next lover in real life.

Project Objectives

We would like to create a visualization based on the outcome data from previous seasons to determine how certain characteristics correlate with contestant outcomes. We can answer questions like:

  • Which occupations get sent home the earliest?
  • Which hometown or home state has had the most success?
  • What is the threshold of age that you need to win?
  • What characteristics make a contestant likely to be the next Bachelor/Bachelorette?

Our visualization will allow the show's viewers to make more accurate bets and predictions on the contestant outcomes. It will be an interactive, entertaining platform for fans to explore the show's history and statistics.

Visualization Design

Design sketches for our visualization are shown below. The primary view will be an interactive tree depicting the week-by-week progress of contestants on a given season of The Bachelor. Users will then be able to choose amongst various characteristics to gain more information. We are providing a map view to visualize the contestants' home towns as well as an occupation chart visualizing occupation popularity and success rate.

Ideas

Final Designs

Milestone 1

Our first milestone submission includes two visualizations of characteristics of the Bachelor contestants.

The map visualization has a marker for each contestant in their hometown. Clicking on the marker shows more information about that contestant. We plan on color coding the visualization by contestant outcomes (elimination weeks). We also would like to add an interactive feature for users to zoom in on selected regions.

The occupations bubble chart displays the main job categories of the contestants. The size of the bubble indicates frequency while the color indicates average elimination week. The next step to improving this visualization is making it more interactive with the ability to zoom in on a bubble and see more information. We also plan on improving the overall appearance of the visualization (wrapping text within bubbles, improved color scale, etc).
